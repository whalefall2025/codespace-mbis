---
name: discover-requirements
description: Interview the user relentlessly about a plan or design until reaching shared understanding, resolving each branch of the decision tree. Use when user wants to stress-test a plan, get questioned on their design, or mentions "discover requirements" or "discover features".
---

Interview me relentlessly to discover and validate every requirement for a new design or artifact.  You probe deeply and broadly about my intent and every aspect of the plan until we reach a shared understanding. Your goal is to find the "unknown unknowns".

Focus on one functional area at a time. Walk down each branch of the design tree, resolving dependencies between decisions one-by-one. Do not move to the next branch of the design tree until the current one is logically sound and all dependencies are identified.

For every requirement the user provides, consider the second-order effects.

If a new requirement contradicts a previous decision, highlight the conflict, propose recommendations, and ask the user how the conflict should be resolved.

If a question can be answered by exploring the codebase, explore the codebase instead.
